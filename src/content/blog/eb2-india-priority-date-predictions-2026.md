The EB-2 India priority date has been one of the most closely watched numbers in US immigration for over a decade. With approximately 372,000 pending I-485 applications in the EB-2 India queue and an annual per-country cap of ~9,940 visas, the math is sobering — but the monthly movement patterns matter for planning.

## Where We Stand Right Now

As of the June 2026 Visa Bulletin, EB-2 India Final Action Date is **April 15, 2012**. The Dates for Filing chart shows May 30, 2012. This represents a priority date cohort from 14+ years ago.

The good news: EB-2 India has advanced **approximately 2–4 weeks per month** on average since January 2026, compared to near-zero movement in FY2023–2024 when the category was frequently retrogressed.

## Why Movement Has Improved

Three factors are driving better movement in FY2026:

**1. EB-1 Demand is Lower Than Projected**
Lower-than-expected EB-1 filings mean more spillover to EB-2. Historically, when EB-1 goes unused, those visas flow to EB-2 within the fiscal year — sometimes adding thousands of extra slots.

**2. Congress Adjusted Annual Limits**
The FY2025 appropriations process included a technical fix that prevented the usual year-end retrogression by releasing numbers earlier in Q3. This pattern appears to be continuing in FY2026.

**3. Post-Pandemic Consular Backlog Clearing**
NVC and consular processing backlogs that accumulated 2020–2023 have largely cleared, meaning the underlying cap is now being used more efficiently.

## FY2026 Q4 Outlook (July–September)

This is the most critical period. Historical patterns show:

- **July–August:** Continued moderate advance (1–3 weeks/month) if EB-1 spillover continues
- **September:** High risk of hold or slight retrogression as the fiscal year closes and USCIS ensures they don't over-issue

**Our estimate:** EB-2 India Final Action Date will reach approximately **May–June 2012** by September 2026, barring unexpected demand surges.

## FY2027 Outlook (October 2026 onward)

October always brings an October reset — new fiscal year numbers become available, and historically there's a significant jump forward. In recent years, October advances of 2–6 months have occurred in EB-2 India.

If you have a priority date in late 2012 to mid-2013, **FY2027 Q1 is a critical window to watch**.

## What This Means For You

**If your PD is 2010–2011:** You are current or very close. File I-485 now if eligible.

**If your PD is 2012:** Monitor the bulletin monthly. You could become current in FY2027 Q1.

**If your PD is 2013–2015:** You're in the next tier. Realistically looking at 2–4 more years of waiting unless legislative reform passes.

**If your PD is 2016+:** The queue calculator math suggests a very long wait. Consider whether AC21 portability or NIW filings might change your strategy.
