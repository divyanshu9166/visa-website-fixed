# Advance Parole (Form I-131) in 2026: Travel Rules, Combo Card & Processing

If you have a pending green card application and need to travel abroad, **advance parole** is what lets you return to the U.S. without abandoning your case. In 2026, filing patterns have shifted — combo cards are increasingly decoupled, and travel timing matters more than ever. This guide explains how **Form I-131** advance parole works, the EAD combo card, fees, processing expectations, and the travel rules that trip people up.

> **Bottom line:** If your **Form I-485** is pending and you leave the U.S. **without an approved advance parole document**, USCIS can treat your green card application as **abandoned**. Never book international travel until you understand your document status.

## What advance parole is

Advance parole is permission, requested on **Form I-131 (Application for Travel Documents, Parole Documents, and Arrival/Departure Records)**, that lets certain applicants return to the U.S. after temporary travel abroad. Per [USCIS](https://www.uscis.gov/i-131), Form I-131 is used to apply for a reentry permit, refugee travel document, TPS travel authorization, an **advance parole document**, and related records.

Advance parole matters most for **adjustment-of-status (AOS) applicants** — people with a pending Form I-485 who are inside the United States and want to travel while they wait.

## The EAD combo card vs. standalone advance parole

When you file Form I-485, you can also file **Form I-765 (work permit)** and **Form I-131 (advance parole)** together. Historically, USCIS issued a single **"combo card"** — an EAD that also served as your advance parole travel document.

In 2026, applicants increasingly see the two **decoupled**:

- **Combo card:** I-765 and I-131 approved together → one card with work + travel authorization.
- **Split approval:** I-765 approved first, so you can **start working sooner**, while I-131 (advance parole) stays pending. In that case the work card is marked *"Not Valid for Reentry,"* and travel authorization arrives later.

The practical upside: you may be able to work earlier. The catch: **do not assume a work-only card lets you travel.**

| Scenario | What you receive | Can you travel? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Combo card approved | Single EAD/AP card | Yes, once card is in hand |
| I-765 approved, I-131 pending | Work card marked "Not Valid for Reentry" | No — wait for advance parole |
| I-131 approved separately | Advance Parole Document (e.g., Form I-512L) | Yes, per the document's validity |

## Fees and how to file

The **Form I-131 filing fee** is **$630** under the current USCIS fee schedule, though some categories and concurrent-filing scenarios differ — always confirm the exact amount on the [USCIS I-131 page](https://www.uscis.gov/i-131) and the official fee schedule before you pay. When filed concurrently with a pending I-485, advance parole is generally requested without a separate additional AP fee in many family- and employment-based cases, but fee rules change, so verify.

## Travel rules that trip people up

1. **Do not travel while I-131 is pending.** Leaving the U.S. before advance parole is approved can cause USCIS to treat the application as **abandoned**.
2. **Do not travel on a "Not Valid for Reentry" work card.** That standalone EAD does not authorize reentry.
3. **Carry the actual document.** Bring your approved advance parole document (or valid combo card) plus your I-485 receipt when you re-enter.
4. **Advance parole is not a guaranteed admission.** It lets you seek parole at the border; CBP still inspects you on return.
5. **Some statuses have special rules.** H-1B and L-1 holders with pending I-485s may travel on their underlying visa in some cases — check your specific situation.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I travel abroad while my I-485 is pending?
Only with a valid advance parole document (or, in some cases, a qualifying underlying visa like H-1B/L-1). Traveling without proper authorization can lead USCIS to treat your I-485 as abandoned.

### How much does Form I-131 cost in 2026?
The filing fee is $630 under the current USCIS fee schedule, but amounts vary by category and filing scenario. Confirm the exact fee on the USCIS I-131 page before paying.

### What is a combo card?
A combo card is a single Employment Authorization Document that also serves as your advance parole travel document, issued when your I-765 and I-131 are approved together.

### My EAD says "Not Valid for Reentry" — can I travel?
No. That marking means the card is a work permit only. You must wait for your advance parole document before traveling internationally.

### How long does advance parole take in 2026?
Processing varies by service center and workload. Check current estimates on our I-131 processing times page and the USCIS processing times hub, and never book non-refundable travel before your document is approved.
