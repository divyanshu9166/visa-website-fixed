# N-400 Processing Time in 2026: Citizenship Timeline, Fees & New Civics Test

Wondering how long the **N-400 processing time in 2026** really is? For most applicants, naturalization now takes roughly **5 to 8 months** from filing to the Oath of Allegiance, though some field offices run longer. This guide breaks down the full timeline, the current filing fees, and the **new 2025 civics test** that applies to anyone who filed on or after October 20, 2025.

> **Quick answer:** The national median for Form N-400 is about **5–6 months** in 2026, but total time can exceed a year in busy field offices. Filing online ($710) is $50 cheaper and generally faster than paper ($760).

## N-400 processing time in 2026 at a glance

| Stage | Typical timeframe (2026) |
| --- | --- |
| Receipt notice (Form N-400 accepted) | 1–4 weeks |
| Biometrics (if required) | 3–6 weeks after receipt |
| Interview + civics/English test | 3–6 months after filing |
| Oath of Allegiance ceremony | Days to a few weeks after approval |
| **Total (filing to citizenship)** | **~5–8 months (varies by field office)** |

Processing speed depends heavily on your local USCIS field office workload. Check the latest official estimate on our [N-400 processing times page](/uscis-processing-times/n-400), and compare across forms on the main [USCIS processing times hub](/uscis-processing-times).

## When you can file the N-400

You can file up to **90 days before** you meet the continuous residence requirement:

- **5 years** as a lawful permanent resident (green card holder), **or**
- **3 years** if you have been married to and living with a U.S. citizen spouse.

You must also meet physical presence, good moral character, age (18+), and English/civics requirements. If you are still working through your green card, see our [green card process guide](/green-card).

## N-400 filing fees in 2026

USCIS filing fees for naturalization are:

| Filing method | Fee (2026) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Online | **$710** | Biometrics included; $50 cheaper; faster case management |
| Paper | **$760** | Biometrics included; required for fee waiver/reduction requests |
| Reduced fee | **$380** | Household income 150–400% of Federal Poverty Guidelines (file by paper) |
| Military (INA §328/§329) | **$0** | No filing fee |
| Fee waiver (Form I-912) | **$0** | For applicants who qualify based on hardship |

Online filing lets you pay, track status, view estimated completion dates, and respond to any Requests for Evidence in one place. Fee waivers and reduced fees must be requested on paper.

## The new 2025 civics test — which version applies to you

USCIS implemented an updated naturalization civics test in 2025. **Your filing date determines which test you take:**

- **Filed before October 20, 2025:** the **2008 civics test** — 10 oral questions, **6 correct to pass**.
- **Filed on or after October 20, 2025:** the **2025 civics test** — the officer asks **20 questions** from a list of **128**, and you must answer **12 correctly**; you fail if you answer 9 or more incorrectly.

### Special provision for older, long-term residents

Applicants who are **65 or older** and have been lawful permanent residents for **20+ years** may study a smaller bank of **20 questions** (10 asked), and may take the test in their language of choice.

Everyone must also demonstrate basic **English** reading, writing, and speaking, unless they qualify for a statutory exemption.

## How to speed up your naturalization timeline

- **File online** to shave time and manage your case digitally.
- **Answer requests for evidence quickly** — delays here are a common bottleneck.
- **Keep your address current** with USCIS so you never miss an interview or oath notice.
- **Prepare early for the correct civics test version** based on your filing date.
- If USCIS does not decide **within 120 days of your interview**, you may have a federal court remedy — consult an immigration attorney.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long does N-400 take in 2026?
Most applicants complete naturalization in about 5–8 months, with a national median near 5–6 months. Some field offices take a year or more, so check your local estimate.

### Is the N-400 fee $710 or $760 in 2026?
It is **$710 if you file online** and **$760 if you file on paper**. Biometrics are included in both, and a reduced $380 fee is available for qualifying incomes.

### Which civics test will I take?
If you filed your N-400 before October 20, 2025, you take the 2008 test (10 questions, 6 to pass). If you filed on or after that date, you take the 2025 test (20 questions asked from 128, 12 correct to pass).

### Can I apply for citizenship before my 5-year anniversary?
Yes. You may file up to 90 days before meeting the 5-year (or 3-year, if married to a U.S. citizen) continuous residence requirement.

### Do I still need biometrics for the N-400?
Many applicants attend a biometrics appointment after filing, though USCIS may reuse existing biometrics in some cases. The fee is already included in your filing fee.
