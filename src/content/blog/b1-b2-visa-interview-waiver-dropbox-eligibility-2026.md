# B1/B2 Visa Interview Waiver (Dropbox) Eligibility in 2026: New Rules Explained

If you are renewing a US visitor visa this year, the **B1/B2 visa interview waiver** — widely known as "dropbox" — works very differently than it did in 2023 or 2024. A September 18, 2025 Department of State update, effective **October 1, 2025**, sharply narrowed who can skip the in-person interview. This guide explains the current dropbox eligibility rules for 2026, who is still exempt, and how to plan around long appointment wait times.

> **Bottom line:** Most nonimmigrant visa applicants — including children under 14 and applicants over 79 — now generally need an in-person interview. The main exception for ordinary travelers is a **B-1/B-2 renewal within 12 months** of the prior visa's expiration, subject to strict conditions.

## What changed in the 2025–2026 interview waiver rules

During the pandemic, the State Department temporarily allowed broad interview waivers, including renewals up to 48 months after expiration. That era is over. The current policy — the **Interview Waiver Update of September 18, 2025**, which supersedes the July 25, 2025 guidance — tightened eligibility twice in one year.

Effective October 1, 2025, all nonimmigrant visa applicants generally require an in-person interview **except** the categories listed below.

## Who is eligible for a B1/B2 interview waiver (dropbox) in 2026

You may qualify to skip the interview only if you meet **all** of the following.

### Core eligibility for B-1/B-2 travelers

- You are **renewing a B-1, B-2, or B1/B2 visa** (or a Border Crossing Card/Foil, BBBCC/BBBCV, for Mexican applicants).
- You apply **within 12 months** of the prior visa's expiration.
- The prior visa was **issued for full validity** at the time of issuance.
- You were **at least 18 years old** when the prior visa was issued.

### Additional conditions that always apply

- You apply in your **country of nationality or usual residence**.
- You have **never been refused a visa** (unless that refusal was later overcome or waived).
- You have **no apparent or potential ineligibility**.

Even if you meet every condition, a **consular officer may still require an in-person interview on a case-by-case basis for any reason**.

## Who must attend an in-person interview

| Applicant situation | In-person interview in 2026? |
| --- | --- |
| First-time B1/B2 applicant | Yes |
| B1/B2 renewal more than 12 months after expiration | Yes |
| Applicant under 18 when prior visa was issued | Yes |
| Child under 14 / applicant over 79 (new B1/B2) | Yes |
| Any prior visa refusal not overcome/waived | Yes |
| B1/B2 renewal within 12 months, all conditions met | Generally eligible for waiver* |

\*Subject to consular officer discretion.

## Other categories still eligible for a waiver

Beyond ordinary travelers, the State Department continues to waive interviews for certain official and diplomatic applicants: visa symbols **A-1, A-2, C-3** (with exceptions for attendants, servants, and personal employees), **G-1 through G-4, NATO-1 through NATO-6, and TECRO E-1**, as well as applicants for diplomatic- or official-type visas. **H-2A** agricultural workers can also renew within 12 months under the same full-validity and age conditions as B1/B2.

## How to apply if you qualify for dropbox

1. Complete Form DS-160 and pay the nonimmigrant visa application (MRV) fee.
2. On the appointment portal, confirm whether you are routed to the interview waiver (dropbox) path based on your answers.
3. Submit your passport, DS-160 confirmation, fee receipt, and a compliant photo at the designated drop-off/courier location.
4. Track passport return through the courier service used in your country.

Always confirm the exact steps on your local **U.S. embassy or consulate website**, because procedures and drop-off logistics differ by country.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is dropbox still available for B1/B2 visas in 2026?
Yes, but only for renewals within 12 months of the prior visa's expiration, when the prior visa was full-validity and you were 18 or older at issuance — and only if you have no prior refusals or ineligibilities.

### Can I use dropbox if my visa expired two years ago?
No. As of October 1, 2025, the renewal waiver window is 12 months from expiration. Renewals outside that window require an in-person interview.

### Do children qualify for the interview waiver?
Generally no. The 2025 update removed the blanket exemptions for applicants under 14 and over 79, so most children applying for B1/B2 now need an interview.

### Does an interview waiver guarantee my visa will be approved?
No. Dropbox only waives the interview requirement. The consular officer still adjudicates eligibility and can request an interview or additional evidence at any time.
